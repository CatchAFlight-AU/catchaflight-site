Flying with Pets? A Guide to United Airlines Pet Travel Services for Australian Travelers


For many Australians, pets are part of the family and leaving them behind during long trips can be difficult. Luckily, United Airlines offers comprehensive pet travel services that allow your furry friends to accompany you on your journey. Whether you’re moving to the USA, going on an extended vacation, or simply bringing your pet along for the adventure, United Airlines makes it possible to fly with your pet safely and comfortably.


In this article, we’ll explore United Airlines’ pet travel services, including how to book pet travel, the different options available, and important guidelines Australian travelers need to know before flying with their pets.


United Airlines PetSafe Program


The PetSafe Program is designed to ensure the safety and comfort of pets traveling in the cargo hold of United Airlines flights. This program is ideal for larger pets that cannot travel in the cabin due to size restrictions.


Key features of the PetSafe Program include:


  • Climate-Controlled Cargo Hold: Pets travel in climate-controlled areas of the aircraft, ensuring their comfort during long-haul flights.
  • Trained Staff: United Airlines staff are trained to handle pets during boarding and deplaning, ensuring their safety and well-being throughout the journey.
  • Tracking and Updates: Pet owners can track their pets in real-time using United’s tracking services, receiving updates on their pet’s location and status during the flight.

Pets in the Cabin


For smaller pets, such as cats, dogs, and rabbits, United Airlines allows pets to travel in the cabin with their owners. Here are the key details you need to know about flying with pets in the cabin:


  • Pet Carrier Requirements: Pets must be placed in an airline-approved pet carrier that fits under the seat in front of you. The carrier should be well-ventilated and spacious enough for your pet to stand, turn around, and lie down comfortably.
  • Size Limitations: The combined weight of the pet and the carrier must not exceed 17 pounds (8 kg).
  • Pet Fees: A fee applies for bringing pets in the cabin, typically $125 USD per flight. This fee may vary depending on the route.
  • In-Cabin Restrictions: Pets must remain inside the carrier for the duration of the flight, and they are not allowed on seats or in emergency exit rows.

Health and Documentation Requirements


Before flying with your pet, it’s important to ensure you have the necessary health certificates and documentation to meet the requirements for both Australia and the USA. United Airlines requires that pets be in good health and up to date on all vaccinations before traveling.


  • Veterinary Health Certificate: A certificate issued by a veterinarian is required within 10 days of your flight. This certifies that your pet is fit to fly and free from contagious diseases.
  • Vaccination Records: Pets must be vaccinated against rabies and other required diseases. You’ll need to provide proof of these vaccinations at the time of booking and upon arrival in the USA.
  • Microchipping: Pets must be microchipped for identification purposes. This is a standard requirement when flying internationally with pets.

Booking Pet Travel with United Airlines


Booking your pet’s travel is a straightforward process, but it’s recommended that you plan well in advance. Here’s how to book pet travel with United Airlines:


  • Contact United Airlines PetSafe: For pets traveling in cargo, you’ll need to contact the PetSafe Program directly to arrange your pet’s travel. Be sure to book early, as space for pets is limited.
  • Add Your Pet to Your Reservation: If your pet is traveling in the cabin, you can add your pet to your reservation online or by calling United Airlines. Ensure that you provide all required documents before your departure.
  • Prepare Your Pet for Travel: Ensure your pet is comfortable in their carrier and has everything they need for the journey. It’s also a good idea to schedule a vet appointment a few days before your flight to ensure your pet is fit for travel.

Tips for Flying with Pets on United Airlines


To ensure a smooth travel experience for both you and your pet, here are some helpful tips for flying with pets on United Airlines:


  • Acclimate Your Pet to Their Carrier: Before the trip, get your pet used to being in their carrier by allowing them to spend time inside it in a calm environment.
  • Feed Your Pet Early: Feed your pet a light meal a few hours before the flight, and avoid feeding them right before takeoff to prevent motion sickness.
  • Pack Essentials: Bring along pet essentials, including water, a portable bowl, extra padding for the carrier, and a favorite toy to keep your pet calm during the flight.
  • Stay Calm: Pets can pick up on their owner’s emotions, so stay calm and relaxed throughout the journey to help keep your pet comfortable.
