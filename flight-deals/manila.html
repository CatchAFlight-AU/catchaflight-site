Frequently Asked Questions about Flights to Manila

Q. When is the best time to fly to Manila?

Catch A Flight recommends flying to Manila during the dry season from December to May. These months offer ideal weather for exploring the city and nearby islands.

Q. Which day is the cheapest to fly to Manila from Australia?

Mid-week flights, especially on Tuesdays and Wednesdays, tend to be the most affordable according to our fare monitoring data.

Q. What time of day is the cheapest to book a flight to Manila?

Our team suggests booking around 3 PM AEST to catch the best fares online to Manila.

Q. Which airlines offer the cheapest flights from Australia to Manila?

Low-cost options often include Cebu Pacific, Philippine Airlines, and AirAsia.

Q. How early should I arrive at the airport for my Manila flight?

You should reach the airport at least 3 hours before departure for international flights to Manila.

Q. What is the name of Manila’s main airport?

Manila is served by Ninoy Aquino International Airport (MNL), the primary international gateway to the Philippines.

Q. How long is the flight from Australia to Manila?

Direct flights from Australia to Manila usually take about 8 to 9 hours depending on the departure city.

Q. What are the top attractions to visit in Manila?

Popular sights in and around Manila include:

  • Intramuros – The Walled City
  • Rizal Park
  • Manila Ocean Park
  • Fort Santiago
  • National Museum of the Philippines
  • Binondo (World’s Oldest Chinatown)
  • SM Mall of Asia

Q. Which airlines fly to Manila from Australia?

Popular airlines flying between Australia and Manila include:

  • Philippine Airlines
  • Cebu Pacific
  • Qantas
  • Jetstar Airways
  • AirAsia
  • Singapore Airlines
